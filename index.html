👗 Tips & Etiquette
🧳
Dress Code, Tipping & Shops
▶
⛪
Churches

Knees and shoulders covered — entry refused otherwise. Carry a light scarf at all times.

👟
Footwear

Flip flops for beach only. Not for towns, restaurants, or churches.

🛍️
Shop Etiquette

Always greet on entry (Buongiorno/Buonasera). Say Arrivederci leaving. Don't touch without asking. Browsing without greeting is rude.

💶
Tipping

Not expected like in the US. Coperto on bill is normal. Large group: 10–20€ cash is generous. Tips cannot go on credit card — cash only.

Free Time in Burgio
Arab-Norman mountain village · "City of Bells & Ceramics"
▶

Founded ~1000 AD on the Sicani Mountains. Name from Arabic "burǧ" (tower). Sicily's only remaining traditional bronze bell foundry is still active here. The 16th-century ceramic tradition continues with unique yellow-and-green majolica.

🚶
"Town of 100 Portals"

Wind through Arab-medieval alleys with 100+ carved stone portals and ceramic-decorated balconies. The Rabato quarter wraps around the castle.

⛪
Chiesa di San Giuseppe

"Sistine Chapel of the Sicani Mountains" — majolica-tiled bell tower + fully frescoed Baroque interior. May be closed in afternoon.

🏰
Castello di Burgio

Walk up to the ruins for panoramic views. Driver likely can't take you to the historic center — you'll walk. Well worth it.
